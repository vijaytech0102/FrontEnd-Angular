🔄 Template-Driven vs Reactive Forms

Feature Template-Driven Reactive
Setup Simple directives in template FormBuilder in component
Complexity Low - Best for simple forms High - For complex forms
Testability Harder to test Easier to test
Data Binding Two-way (ngModel) One-way (programmatic)
Dynamic Fields Difficult Easy (FormArray)
Async Validators Limited support Full support
Custom Validators Possible but limited Full flexibility
Performance Slower for large forms Optimized for performance

💡 Pro Tips

  • Template-Driven: Best for simple, quick forms. Two-way binding makes development fast.
  • Reactive: Best for complex forms. More control and better testability.
  • Validation: Use built-in validators first, then custom validators as needed.
  • Performance: For large forms, Reactive Forms are more performant.
  • Testing: Reactive Forms are much easier to unit test than Template-Driven.
  • Async: Use async validators for server-side validation (email exists, username taken, etc.).
